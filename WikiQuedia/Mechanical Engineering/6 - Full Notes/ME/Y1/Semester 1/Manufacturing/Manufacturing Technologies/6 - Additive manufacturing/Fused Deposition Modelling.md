# Fused Deposition Modelling
### Process
Fused deposition modelling (FDM), also known as _fused filament fabrication (FFF)_, more commonly known as it's parent category, 3D printing, is a [3D printing](Manufacturing%20Technologies%20Overview#Terms%20and%20Disambiguation) process that layers liquified plastic filament over one another to create a 3D shape. It uses a printer head that moves in 3 dimensions that heats the filament, to make it soft and pliable, to print the product, while the table is stationary. 
This process is only suitable for thermoplasts and no other material. 
##### Use cases
It's a common process for prototyping stages, and, depending on the quality of the printer and the speed of the printer head, is also sometimes used for production of parts that bear almost no loads and would otherwise require some type of [injection molding](injection%20molding) process to manufacture.

### Characteristics
This process is for __thermoplastics__ only.
